My Journey to Co‑author

How casual shayari turned into more than fifty published anthologies.

There was a time when I scribbled poems and shayari just to impress girls and be different among friends with the entitlement of “Shayar”. Honestly, I was naive about my unique skill. In school, I recited my poems on stage and, by receiving applause from students and teachers, I fulfilled my ego without doubt. In those days, I was quite popular among girls, and that was enough for me. Steadily, I passed out of school as well as junior college, carrying only a vague vision of becoming a published author, with no clear plan or source to make it real.

At the beginning of my graduation, my Hindi professor, Dr. Prerna Ubale ma’am, asked me to write a proper poem for the notice board and the college magazine. That is when I truly began to learn what poetry is. It became a turning point for my writing. I discovered new writers, new genres of poetry, different kinds of literature, and much more. Slowly, a new world of writing opened in front of me, each layer like a new realm to explore. I finally wrote a Hindi poem about friendship, “Yaara teri dosti…”, and from there I started exploring literature in English, Urdu, Sanskrit, Hindi and Marathi.

During my final year of Bachelor of Arts, I began posting my poems on Instagram. One day, a message arrived from a stranger named Chandana Kashipuram. It was her first anthology as a compiler, and she invited me to write on “mother’s love”. With a full heart, I accepted and wrote an English poem titled “First Meet”. It was my first ever English poem, a couplet‑style piece that began with the lines about an ocean of love and a seashore of care.

That anthology became my first published work under the pen name “Devil Barewar”, a name I still use for every piece of literature. Since then, the journey has grown far beyond that first invitation. Today, I stand as a co‑author of more than fifty published anthologies, each one a reminder that a casual shayari phase once quietly turned into a lifelong relationship with writing.
